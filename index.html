What is Cicada 3301?

Cicada 3301 is one of the most elaborate and mysterious internet puzzles ever created. It began on January 4, 2012, when an anonymous post appeared on the /b/ board of 4chan. The message was simple: "Hello. We are looking for highly intelligent individuals. To find them, we have devised a test."

Nobody knew who posted it. Nobody knew what it was for. But what followed was a global, multi-layered puzzle hunt spanning cryptography, steganography, ancient literature, number theory, philosophy, and even physical locations around the world. Thousands of people tried to solve it. Very few made it through.

The puzzles required solvers to decode messages hidden inside images, crack Vigenère ciphers using prime-number keys, translate runic text from a mysterious book called Liber Primus, find physical posters in cities across multiple countries, and navigate Tor hidden services that appeared and disappeared at precise times.

Cicada appeared three times — in 2012, 2013, and 2014. Those who completed each hunt were contacted privately. What they were told, and by whom, remains unknown to this day. The identity of Cicada 3301 has never been confirmed. Liber Primus — a handwritten runic book that is central to the mystery — remains only partially decoded as of 2026.

Timeline of events
January 4, 2012
The first post
An anonymous image is posted to 4chan's /b/ board with the text: "Hello. We are looking for highly intelligent individuals." Hidden inside the image's metadata is the first clue — a Caesar-shifted message pointing solvers to a Reddit link.
January 2012
Images within images
Using the Outguess steganography tool, solvers extract hidden data from JPEG images. Each decoded image contains another layer — book ciphers, prime-number keys, and Base64 strings. The trail leads to a Tor hidden service that activates at a specific date and time.
January 9, 2012
Physical posters appear worldwide
Posters bearing a QR code and the Cicada cicada image appear simultaneously in cities including Warsaw, Paris, Seoul, Sydney, Miami, and Tucson. Solvers photograph and share them online. The QR codes lead to more encrypted messages.
March 2012
Winners contacted — Hunt 1 ends
Those who completed the final steps of the 2012 puzzle are contacted privately via PGP-signed email. What they were told and what they were recruited for remains secret. Cicada then goes silent.
January 4, 2013
Hunt 2 begins
Exactly one year after the first post, Cicada returns with a new image on 4chan. The 2013 puzzle is more complex — it introduces Vigenère ciphers with prime-derived keys, deeper use of steganography, and new physical locations. Thousands attempt it; very few finish.
April 2013
Liber Primus introduced
Cicada releases Liber Primus — a 58-page book written entirely in Elder Futhark runes. It is the central artifact of the entire mystery. Most of it remains undeciphered. Solvers believe it contains the core message or ideology of whoever is behind Cicada 3301.
January 4, 2014
Hunt 3 — the last confirmed round
The third and final confirmed Cicada puzzle launches. It is the most philosophically dense, referencing Aleister Crowley, Jungian archetypes, and deep cryptographic theory. Several more pages of Liber Primus are unlocked. No further official hunt has been confirmed since.
2015 – 2016
Unconfirmed activity
Possible Cicada activity appears in 2015 and 2016, but none is verified via PGP signature — the method Cicada always used to authenticate messages. The community remains divided on whether these were genuine or imitations.
2016 – Present
Liber Primus — still unsolved
The global community of solvers continues to work on the remaining pages of Liber Primus. Some pages have been partially decoded. Others remain completely opaque. Whoever — or whatever — Cicada 3301 was, they have not officially returned. The puzzle remains open.
